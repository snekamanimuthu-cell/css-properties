Space is vast and mostly empty. The observable universe is estimated to be about 93 billion light-years in diameter. Stars are born in clouds of gas and dust called nebulae. Our solar system is just one of billions in the Milky Way galaxy. Black holes are regions where gravity is so strong that even light cannot escape. Planets orbit stars, and moons orbit planets. Galaxies collide and merge over billions of years, creating new stars.
